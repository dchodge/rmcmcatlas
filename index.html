ESSENTIAL TOOLS FOR ANALYZING MCMC OUTPUT, ASSESSING CONVERGENCE, AND CREATING PUBLICATION-QUALITY VISUALIZATIONS OF POSTERIOR DISTRIBUTIONS.

CONVERGENCE DIAGNOSTICS

coda summary.mcmc()

Comprehensive MCMC diagnostics and summaries

posterior summarise_draws()

Modern posterior analysis with tidy data principles

VISUALIZATION & PLOTTING

tidybayes spread_draws()

Tidy data manipulation for Bayesian models

ggdist stat_halfeye()

Visualizations of distributions and uncertainty

QUICK START EXAMPLE

# Load packages
library(coda)
library(posterior)
library(tidybayes)
library(ggdist)

# Basic diagnostics
summary(mcmc_samples)
plot(mcmc_samples)

# Modern analysis
posterior_samples <- as_draws_matrix(mcmc_samples)
summarise_draws(posterior_samples)

# Tidy visualization
tidy_samples <- spread_draws(posterior_samples, var1, var2)
ggplot(tidy_samples, aes(x = var1)) + 
  stat_halfeye() + 
  theme_minimal()

About the MCMC Atlas

The MCMC Atlas was created to provide a centralized, comprehensive resource for anyone working with Markov Chain Monte Carlo methods in R. Our goal is to make it easy to discover, compare, and choose the right MCMC algorithm for your specific needs.

This atlas is maintained by the R MCMC community and welcomes contributions from researchers, practitioners, and developers worldwide.
